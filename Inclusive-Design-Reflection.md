## Reflection
### How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?
* I will focus on providing clear, written documentation for all tasks. Knowing that I personally value predictability, I will ensure that any requests I make include the "why" and a clear "Definition of Done." I will also be mindful of my "monotone" exterior during meetings, ensuring I use written follow-ups to express my support and engagement with the team's ideas.

### What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?
* Using vague instructions like 'fix this when you can' or 'make it look better'.
* Using overly complex language or metaphors that might be taken literally.

### What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?
* I will adopt a "No Surprise" policy. This means mapping out tasks in GitHub issues well in advance and providing at least 15–30 minutes of notice before a significant shift in priority or an unscheduled meeting.

## Task
### Identify one design or communication improvement that could make Focus Bear more accessible. 

* Based on my experience with planning-related anxiety, I suggest adding an **Advance Notice Feature** to Focus Bear. This would provide a customizable 5-to-10 minute warning before a focus block ends or a new activity starts. 

### Practice writing a clear, patient, and supportive response to a hypothetical user struggling with executive functioning

* Hi! I hear that the sudden transition between your focus blocks felt stressful. I completely understand how disruptive an unplanned change can feel. To help make things more predictable, you might want to try enabling the 'Transition Warning' in your settings. This gives you a 5-minute heads-up so you can finish your current thought before the next block begins. Let me know if that helps!
