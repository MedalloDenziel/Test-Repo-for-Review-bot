# Professionalism at Focus Bear -Denziel Medallo
## Reflection
### Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?
* I’ve seen great teamwork when everyone follows a consistent workflow. It’s effective because it removes confusion, when everyone knows where a task stands, the team can move forward without waiting for constant status updates.
### What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?
* I will use the "15-minute rule" for urgent pings to respect people's focus time. I will also provide clear context in my messages so my teammates don't have to guess my intent, ensuring I’m being helpful rather than demanding.
### How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?
* I can be an active listener in meetings. Even if I don't have much to say, showing that I am processing the topic and following up with a written "thank you" or a summary of action items helps build a supportive atmosphere.
### How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?
* I will check my emails and messages the night before to ensure I’m up to date. I’ll also prepare a short list of "blocked" items or questions so I can make the most of everyone’s time.
### What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?
* To me, being proactive means updating my progress on GitHub issues before being asked. It also means reaching out when I'm stuck *after* I've tried to solve the problem myself, so my supervisor knows I'm making an effort.
### How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?
* I would send a polite message on Discord. If I don't get a response within a reasonable timeframe (e.g., 24 hours for non-urgent items), I would send a follow-up email. I would only escalate to a supervisor if a task is "blocking" my entire daily plan and I’ve received no response across multiple channels.
### What are some things you won't do?
* I won't leave tasks "in progress" on GitHub without an update for days.
* I won't ignore messages, even if I'm busy (I'll at least send a "will check this soon" note).
* I won't be afraid to admit when I don't know something.
