## Reflection
### What do you think are the biggest benefits and challenges of Agile?
* The biggest benefit is flexibility; Agile allows us to pivot quickly based on feedback rather than following a rigid, outdated plan. The biggest challenge is "scope creep"—because the plan is flexible, it’s easy for a small task to turn into a large one if the requirements aren't tightly managed.
### ow can Agile principles help improve your role and responsibilities?
* Agile principles, specifically transparency, help me stay aligned with the team. By using a Kanban board, I can see exactly what is high priority, which prevents me from wasting time on tasks that aren't currently needed. It supports my need for clear requirements and structured work.

## Task 
### Summarize the main differences between Scrum and Kanban in your own words.
* **Scrum** is based on "Sprints", fixed time blocks where a set amount of work must be finished and it has specific roles like a Scrum Master.
* **Kanban** is a continuous flow. There are no fixed start/stop dates for a "sprint"; instead, we focus on moving tasks through a "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done" column as quickly as possible. It limits "Work in Progress" (WIP) to keep the team focused.
## dentify one Agile principle you think will be most useful in your work.
* **"Simplicity—the art of maximizing the amount of work not done—is essential."** As a developer, this is my favorite principle. It reminds me to focus on building exactly what is needed for the user without over-complicating the code or adding "fancy" features that weren't requested. It keeps my workflow clean and efficient.
